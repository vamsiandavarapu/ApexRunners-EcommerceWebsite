OUR STORY

More than a shoe. It's a symbol of greatness, defying gravity since 1984.

1984

The Beginning

Michael Jordan signs with Nike. The Apex Runner 1 is born, changing sneaker culture forever despite being banned by the NBA for its bold colors.

1988

The Jumpman Era

Tinker Hatfield introduces the Apex Runner 3, featuring the iconic Jumpman logo and visible Air unit, solidifying the brand's premium status.

1996

The Flu Game

Jordan battles through illness in the Finals wearing the Apex Runner 12, elevating the shoe to legendary status.

2024

40 Years of Legacy

Four decades later, Apex Runner remains the pinnacle of basketball performance and streetwear culture globally.
